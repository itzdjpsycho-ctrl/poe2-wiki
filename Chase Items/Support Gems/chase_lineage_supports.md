# Chase Lineage Support Gems — Path of Exile 2
*Stats verified against poe2db.tw (Patch 0.5)*

---

## What Are Lineage Support Gems?

Lineage support gems are a special tier of support gems introduced in Path of Exile 2. Key rules:
- Each gem requires **Level 65** to equip
- Each gem belongs to a named **family/category** (e.g. "Uhtred of Kalguur", "Spell Echo")
- A skill can only socket **one Lineage gem per category** — two gems from the same family cannot both support the same skill
- All are **boss-exclusive drops** (most require specific pinnacle or anomaly bosses)
- They cannot be chanced

There are 77 Lineage supports total. Listed here are the chase- and build-enabling tier gems.

## Rigwald's Ferocity
**Level Req:** 65 | **Support Req:** +5 Dexterity | **Category:** Dual Wield | **~150 Divine Orbs**

### Stats
- **Weapon Set I:** Supported Skills have 30% increased Attack Speed, deal 15% less Damage
- **Weapon Set II:** Supported Skills have 10% reduced Attack Speed, deal 30% more Damage
- Supports Attack skills; cannot modify Minion skills
- Mana Cost: 100%

### Why It's Powerful
- Lets attack builds use two distinct modes on demand — fast clear speed via Set I, then burst single-target via Set II
- The 30% more Damage in Set II is a multiplicative modifier, not additive — it stacks with all other damage bonuses
- Weapon-swapping between encounters or during boss phases becomes a genuine DPS rotation tool
- One of the most universally applicable Lineage gems for any attack build

| **Varloch, the Ashen Lord** | Boss drop | Either boss can drop it |

## Seraph's Heart
**Level Req:** 65 | **Category:** Persistent Buff | **Spirit Reservation:** 45 | **~130 Divine Orbs**

### Stats
- Supports Persistent Buff Skills (Heralds, Auras, Defensive Buffs)
- "Enemy Hits have 20% chance to treat all your Resistances as being 90% while a Supported Skill is active"

- Does not require building around a specific playstyle; plug into any character with a persistent buff
- Stacks implicitly with actual resistance values — the effective floor goes up if your resistances are already high

## Ixchel's Torment
**Level Req:** 65 | **Category:** Fluke | **Cost Multiplier:** 120% | **~10.7 Divine Orbs**

### Stats
- Supports skills that Trigger other Skills
- Triggered skills deal between **40% less and 60% more Damage** (random per trigger)
- Triggered skills gain between **40% less and 50% more Area of Effect** (random per trigger)
- Triggered skills gain between **40% less and 50% more Projectile Speed** (random per trigger)
- Triggered skills recover between 0% and 20% of cost on use

### Why It's Powerful
- On lucky rolls (which happen constantly), triggered skills deal 60% more damage and have a 50% larger area — these are enormous bonuses
- For builds using many triggered skills per second, the average outcome is net positive due to how often peak rolls occur in rapid succession
- The cost recovery on use partially offsets the 120% cost multiplier
- Drops from The Trialmaster — farmable through high Ascendancy Trials

### How to Obtain
| Method | Reliability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **The Trialmaster** | Boss drop | Requires completing high-tier Trials of Chaos |
| **Player Trade** | ~10.7 Divine | Solid demand from trigger spell and totem builds |

## Rakiata's Flow
**Level Req:** 65 | **Category:** Inversion | **Support Req:** +5 Dexterity | **Cost Multiplier:** 120%

### Stats
- Supports Skills that can cause Damaging Hits (269 compatible skills)
- "Hits with Supported Skills treat Enemy Monster Elemental Resistance values as inverted"

- Works on all elemental damage types simultaneously
- One of the highest flat damage multipliers in the game against resistant enemies; BiS for any elemental hit build that doesn't have access to penetration stacking

## Eonyr's Thunder
**Level Req:** 65 | **Category:** Electrocute | **Support Req:** +5 Dexterity | **Introduced:** Patch 0.5.0

### Stats
- Supports Lightning skills that Hit enemies
- Lightning damage from Supported Skills can inflict Electrocute
- "Supported Skills Cannot inflict Shock" (Electrocute replaces Shock)
- When Electrocuted enemies are slain, they explode via "Voltaic Fulmination":
  - 5–14% of the corpse's max life as Lightning damage
  - Explosion radius 1.5–2.1 metres
  - 0.20–0.30 second cooldown (up to 5 uses)

### Why It's Powerful
- Electrocute is a stronger crowd-control effect than Shock — enemies are paralysed entirely, not just slowed
- The corpse explosion chaining is a built-in clear AoE bonus — electrocuted enemies die and blast nearby foes
- Lightning builds that previously stacked Shock now gain a completely different and arguably stronger status effect
- Synergises directly with Breachlord's Rift (break armour on electrocute)

### How to Obtain
| Method | Reliability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **General drop (level 52+)** | Uncommon | Specific drop source not confirmed on poe2db.tw |
| **Player Trade** | Moderate | Key gem for lightning electrocute builds |

## Breachlord's Amalgam
**Level Req:** 65 | **Category:** Returning Projectiles | **~2.5 Divine Orbs**

### Stats
- Supports Lightning or Cold damage skills that fire Projectiles (no existing cooldown)
- "Projectiles from Supported Skills Return to you"
- "Returning Projectiles from Supported Skills Pierce all targets"
- Cooldown Time: 6.00 seconds (3 uses)
- Mana Cost: 100%
- Introduced in Patch 0.5.0

### Why It's Powerful
